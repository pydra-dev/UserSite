Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

The Pydra Project (herein referred to as “Pydra”) takes privacy very seriously. This Privacy Policy outlines the conditions under which Pydra may access or monitor data hosted on hardware we provide.

Data Access

Pydra will never access any data hosted on our hardware, except in the following situation:

  • We have reasonable suspicion of illegal activity or a violation of our Terms of Use.
  • We receive a valid request from law enforcement or a government authority.
  • We identify behavior that triggers our automated security tools as described below.

Automated Monitoring

We use automated monitoring tools to detect Process IDs associated with known malicious or prohibited applications.

This monitoring does not involve reading or analyzing your hosted content.

If a violation is detected, we may take action, including suspension, and reserve the right to investigate further as needed.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties, except as legally required or as outlined in these terms.

Data Retention

If your access to Pydra hosted hardware is suspended or terminated, all data stored on the hardware will be deleted after 14 days, unless required to preserve it by law enforcement or other legal obligations.

If you would like to obtain a copy of your data before deletion, you may contact the Pydra team to request a copy within 14 days of the initial suspension or termination.

Policy Updates

This policy may be updated periodically. Changes will be posted on our website, and your continued use implies acceptance.
